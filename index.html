Simple starting points for a better cup at home.

Grind
Medium, like kosher salt
Ratio
About 55-60 g per liter
Full 12-cup pot
About 95-105 g coffee, depending on machine size

Drip coffee is the classic home coffee method. Use a medium grind, a clean filter, and filtered water if possible. For a full 12-cup pot, start around 95-105 grams of coffee. If the cup tastes weak or watery, use a little more coffee or grind slightly finer. If it tastes bitter, use slightly less coffee or grind coarser.

NCA describes drip coffee as a common starting point because it is convenient, consistent, and good for larger batches. It also recommends a medium grind for drip coffee and notes that a typical drip brew has about five minutes of contact time.

Grind
Coarse, like rock salt
Ratio
About 1:12 to 1:16
Time
About 4 minutes

French press makes a heavier, richer cup because the coffee steeps directly in the water and is filtered with a metal screen. Use a coarse grind, steep for about 4 minutes, then press gently and pour right away. Do not leave the coffee sitting in the press, or it can keep extracting and turn bitter.

NCA gives French press a broad ratio range of 1:10 to 1:16, recommends water around 93 +/- 3 C, and gives an approximate brew time of 4 minutes.

Grind
Already handled inside the pod
Best setting
Smaller cup size for stronger coffee

For the best K-cup flavor, use the 6 oz or 8 oz setting if your machine allows it. Larger settings can make the coffee taste thin because the same amount of coffee is being stretched across more water. For a stronger cup, use a smaller brew size rather than brewing the same pod twice.

For Millionaire's Roast K-cups, the key advice is simple: smaller brew size means better flavor.

Pour-over gives you more control than an automatic drip machine. Use a medium grind, rinse the paper filter, add your coffee, then pour a small amount of hot water over the grounds and let it bloom for 30-45 seconds. Continue pouring slowly until you reach your target amount of water. Total brew time should usually land around 2-4 minutes.

NCA describes pour-over as a manual version of drip brewing that gives the brewer more control, with quick numbers of 1:13 to 1:16, 93 +/- 3 C, and 2-4 minutes contact time.

About 12 hours

Cold brew is made by steeping coarse-ground coffee in cool or room-temperature water. For a concentrate, use about 1 part coffee to 4-5 parts water, steep for about 12 hours, then strain. Serve over ice and dilute with water, milk, or creamer to taste.

NCA gives cold brew quick numbers of 1:4 to 1:5, room temperature or cooler water, and about 12 hours of contact time.

Quick answers

Short and useful, so ordering stays easy.

Use our online shop to choose shipping, pickup, or delivery in one place.

Millionaire's Roast is roasted in Beardstown and regularly serves Springfield and the wider Central Illinois area.

We currently offer three fresh single-origin coffees: Costa Rican Tarrazú Jaguar Honey as our flagship medium roast that is here to stay, plus Peruvian Chonta G1 Washed and Brazilian Cerrado Natural as rotating origins in the medium dark and dark slots. Every coffee in the lineup is specialty grade, selected from coffees that cup 80+, and available as whole bean, ground, or K-Cups.

We source green coffee in bulk, but harvest timing differs by region and crop cycle. Once a coffee sells through, there is a high chance that specific lot is gone for now, not forever, and the menu keeps rotating based on demand and availability.

Specialty-grade coffee starts with better green coffee before it ever reaches the roaster. Our coffees are selected from documented 80+ lots, meaning they have been evaluated for cup quality rather than treated like ordinary commodity coffee. In the coffee industry, coffees are assessed through cupping: a standardized tasting process that looks at things like aroma, flavor, aftertaste, acidity, body, balance, sweetness, cleanliness, and defects. A score of 80 or higher is the traditional threshold for specialty-grade coffee. For customers, the result is simple: cleaner flavor, clearer tasting notes, and a more intentional cup.
